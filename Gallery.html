Inside a Mercedes Sprinter


Recycling old wood
The majority of vans are built with new materials but what I’ve tried to focus on is using defunct furniture that folk are scrapping all the time; tables and wardbrobes can go a long way for parts inside a van. Save it, repurpose it and enjoy telling the story! This camper has all the normal functionality of a campervan so I won't go into those boring bits, although it does have a barometer! In this van my favourite is the sheesham Indian hardwood which makes up the worktop, drawers and clothes shelves; all from a £20 table. The rest is made up of American Cherry, English Ash and Pine.

Tap

The tap is a simple piece of copper pipe I made up just using a gas torch, gradually moving the flame to heat the pipe and produce a gentle bend. The water pump is turned on using the small electric latching switch behind the tap which illuminates when on.
×

Homemade Stove

The stove is made from the reminants of an old rusty 60’s camping stove. I used to make race exhausts pipes for motorbikes so had some titanium sheet in my garage. It’s the best for corrosion resistance and looks elegant because it is so thin. The wire supports are made from 5mm stainless rods which I bent using a gas torch to get the shape I wanted and then welded them to some threaded bosses I made on my lathe. The structure is then bolted from underneath to produce a clean finish and most importantly it doesn’t rattle when you’re driving!
